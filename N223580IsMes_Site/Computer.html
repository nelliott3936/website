Many enthusiasts argue that "Old Apple" (the era roughly spanning the return of Steve Jobs in 1997 to his passing in 2011) was superior because it prioritized singular vision and radical simplicity over market saturation. During this period, the product lineup was lean and easy to understand, following the "four-quadrant" philosophy that made choosing a computer effortless. There was a sense of "insanely great" craftsmanship where every detail—from the tactile "click" of an iPod scroll wheel to the glowing Apple logo on a MacBook—felt like a deliberate piece of art rather than a calculated corporate iteration. While modern Apple is undeniably a more efficient and powerful services giant, the older era felt more like a boutique rebellion, driven by the pursuit of the "perfect" device rather than the pursuit of a diversified ecosystem.   -Gemini
